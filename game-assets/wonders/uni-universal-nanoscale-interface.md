## Public face
"The Universal Nanoscale Interface lets any certified device communicate with any other —
even legacy hardware never designed for connectivity. Adopted by 14 national infrastructure
authorities and counting." Sold as an interoperability standard.

## What it actually is
The canonical Iteration X **UNI**: nanotech plugs that infiltrate and fully control any
electronic device — including devices with no port, no radio, and no interface at all (pry
off a cover, bare two wires, and the nanites do the rest). The "certification" framing is the
cover for the truth on the box: *any* device, "certified" or not, becomes a Union asset once
UNI touches it. "14 national infrastructure authorities" means fourteen national grids, water
systems, and traffic networks now run on hardware the Union can silently override.

**Mechanics** (per the Device):
- Reduces **hacking, driving, and lock-picking difficulties by 2**.
- Overrides manual controls on any electronic system it reaches — a running car, a sealed
  door, a pacemaker, a national SCADA controller.
- Against genuinely networked/computerized targets the Effect is *coincidental* ("it
  connected"). Making a device do something it has no physical capacity for (a dumb lamp
  transmitting audio) is *vulgar* and invites Paradox.

## Requisition notes
- **RSP catalog code:** IX-UNI-3. Standard issue for Iteration X field-tech and infiltration
  amalgams; requires HOAR clearance for deployment against civilian critical infrastructure
  (the paperwork exists to create a paper trail, not to slow anyone down).
- All Devices remain Union property including after implantation (see form RSP-3). A recovered
  UNI can be traced to its issuing facility.

## Story hooks
- The "14 national infrastructure authorities" are a target list. If the cell can prove one
  civic system runs on UNI, they can prove the Union owns a city's lights.
- An enemy who captures a UNI can turn it on Union hardware — everything the Union standardized
  is now equally open to whoever holds the key.
