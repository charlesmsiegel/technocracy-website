## Public face
There is none. This hardware never had a legitimate consumer story; the casings *claim* to be
"consumer-adjacent prototype hardware," but per the Syndicate's own assay, "none of which does
what its casing says it does." The public knows nothing. Internally it is an accounting
liability with a body count.

## What it actually is
Legacy materiel built by the **Special Projects Division** before the Division went dark after
the Dimensional Anomaly (1999). The units leach **Reality-Toxin** — the physical residue of
magick worked against a hostile Consensus, the same substance that poisons Deviants and taints
Wonders. Two damning findings on the LEDGER board:

- **The inventory is ripening, not decaying.** Leach rates rise ~14%/year (sy-06). Whatever
  Pattern is inside is becoming *more* active with age, not less — consistent with a Wonder
  bound to something that does not obey entropy.
- **Nephandic provenance.** SISD's off-book review (sy-08) ties SPD procurement to Nephandic
  materiel, live human testing, and "corrupted Deviant-origin components." These are not
  Enlightened Devices. They are Fallen work wearing Union casings.

**Mechanics.**
- **Exposure:** handling limit **40 minutes per technician per month** (assay-confirmed).
  Within the limit: irritability, "vivid product-endorsement dreams," and a compulsion to
  describe the inventory as "wasted on storage" — a low-grade **Mind/Entropy** suggestion
  radiating off the objects, steering handlers toward *using* them. Treat as a difficulty +1
  to resist tampering per exposure; failure = the technician starts arguing to activate a unit.
- **Over-exposure / activation:** Storyteller's discretion, catastrophic. Reality-Toxin causes
  aggravated Pattern damage and Paradox on contact with active magick. Thermal destruction is
  contraindicated — Phase 2 incineration of Batch 28 produced a district-wide outbreak of
  "enthusiastic brand loyalty toward products that do not exist" (sy-09).
- **Approved disposal ("archiving"):** dissolve each unit's Pattern over **40 days in a Primal
  Utility (Prime) sink**. Slow, expensive, quiet. "Nothing leaves early. Nothing is salvaged
  for parts."

## Requisition notes
- **Cannot be requisitioned.** Access is restricted to the Rotterdam archiving detail under
  Comptroller Voss (sy-09), badge-lockout enforced. Batch 31 is assay/CCC only.
- Any operative caught salvaging a unit is a personnel problem, not a gear problem.
