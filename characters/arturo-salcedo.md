## Sleeper Notes (no Spheres, no Enhancement)
The man who empties the bins around the most-watched sky on Earth and has never once looked at a screen he wasn't cleaning. Arturo works nights because they're quiet and the pay is fair. He knows the building better than the building's managers do — which stairwell floods, which door sticks, which console officer (Tomas) hasn't been sleeping. He is exactly what the Perimeter floor needs and cannot requisition: someone genuinely, unshakeably trustworthy who wants nothing from them.

## Willpower
6

## Backgrounds
- Resources 1, Contacts 2 (every night-shift worker on campus), Loyalty (rated, by an instrument, at a level reserved for people who have been *given a reason*)

## Merits & Flaws
- **Merit: Common Sense** — He notices the human things the instruments miss: who's grieving, who's lying, who left the coffee on.
- **Flaw: Illiterate in the Union's language** — He has cleaned this floor for eleven years and understood not one classified thing on it, which is the only reason he's still allowed to.

## Description & Demeanor
Gentle, unhurried, quick with a nod and slow with words; keeps a transistor radio and a thermos and a photograph laminated against the damp. He greets the console officers by name, tells Tomas to eat something, and treats the whole humming apparatus of DEEP PERIMETER as just a very expensive building that needs its floors done. The night crew love him without quite noticing they do.

## What They Want
For the young ones on the night shift to eat, sleep, and get home safe — and to keep the job long enough to send the last of the money home.

## Secret
Twenty-two years ago Void Engineering's Search & Rescue brought his brother home from an "industrial accident" that no one would explain — brought him *home*, when everyone said there'd be nothing to bring. Arturo doesn't know what the company is. He knows only that they returned his brother's body when they didn't have to, and he has quietly, gratefully cleaned their floors ever since. That is the whole of the anomalous loyalty score, and no instrument on the campus can read what it actually says.
