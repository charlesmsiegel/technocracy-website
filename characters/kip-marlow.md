## Sleeper Notes (no Spheres, no Enhancement)
The perfect Mercury courier: fast, reliable, and trained not to wonder. Kip runs a Plexic-routed handset that computes delivery paths "shorter than the naive geometry would suggest," and Kip has stopped questioning why the route sometimes says to turn down an alley that comes out three blocks farther than it should. The packages get there. Twice, the package arrived before the confirmation ping. Kip files these under "good day."

*Issued gear (Union property, not a Background): the Plexic-routed courier handset — a Technocratic Trinket, which Kip does not realize is the interesting part.*

## Merits & Flaws
- **Merit: Perfect Sense of Direction** — Kip never gets lost, even on the routes that shouldn't connect.
- **Flaw: Incurious by Policy** — Certified, contractual, and by now genuinely internalized. Kip's certification bonus is explicitly for *not asking*, and Kip has come to like not asking.

## Description & Demeanor
Wiry, cheerful, always slightly out of breath; earbuds in, helmet on, treats the whole city as a physics problem Kip is winning. Kip is proud of a perfect delivery record and tells friends the job is "basically teleportation with a bike," not knowing how close to the mark that is. Kip is kind to the doormen and never once peeks in a package.

## What They Want
To keep the perfect record, make rent, and someday afford the electric cargo bike with the good motor.

## Secret
One night Kip delivered a sealed case to a set of GPS coordinates in the middle of a bridge — no building, no person, just a spot on the railing — and when Kip turned to leave, the case was already gone and the confirmation had already pinged. Kip has taken that route home every night since, slowing at the same railing, and has never once seen who takes the packages. Kip has decided, firmly, not to find out.
