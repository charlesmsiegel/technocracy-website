## Sleeper Notes (no Spheres, no Enhancement)
The face of an office that barely exists. Gloria greets a stream of couriers, contractors, and consultants who never seem to be the same person twice, buzzes them through, and signs for crates addressed to a floor she's never been asked to visit. She has a warm word for everyone and a name for every regular. She is, without knowing it, the most reliable part of the LEDGER vacancy buffer.

## Willpower
5

## Backgrounds
- Resources 2, Contacts 3 (every courier and building-services worker on the block)

## Merits & Flaws
- **Merit: Everybody's Friend** — Strangers relax the second she smiles; visitors sign in honestly without knowing why.
- **Flaw: Incurious by Habit** — She stopped wondering about the locked elevator years ago. It was easier, and the raises kept coming.

## Description & Demeanor
Bright, maternal, endlessly patient; keeps hard candy in a bowl and photos of her grandkids taped to the monitor. She's worked this desk for nineteen years, longer than anyone in the building, and treats the strange comings-and-goings as simply "how a big company works." Everyone who passes her desk leaves a little calmer, which is exactly the service the front was built to provide.

## What They Want
To make it to a full pension, spoil her grandchildren, and finally get maintenance to fix the elevator to the eleventh floor.

## Secret
Once a quarter, exactly four days after the building's quietest week, a single envelope arrives for the sealed floor with no return address and no courier — it is simply *there* in her inbound tray when she comes back from lunch. She has never opened one. She signs the log "received," every time, because the one time she left the box blank, her paycheck came four days early.
