## Enhancement & Gear (in place of Spheres)
- **No Enhancement dots, no cyberware.** Wynn's edge is a native, un-Enlightened aptitude — "an unusual relationship with luck," in the words of the Exceptional Talent Assessment Program. Wynn reads a room's wants the way others read a ticker and has a preternatural, statistically inexplicable win rate on any wager Wynn actually intends to win.
- **Signature gear** — a Syndicate-issued EDG Virtuous 8 (whose Desire Engine Wynn distrusts and outperforms), a bespoke wardrobe from Eris Design Group, a black card, and a debt ledger the desk reads as "a strong indicator."
- **Genius:** none. The Syndicate has flagged Wynn for possible Epiphany and is watching to see whether the aptitude ever tips over into actual Enlightenment.

## Merits & Flaws
- **Merit: Luck** — Once per session, reroll a failed roll on anything Wynn has staked something real against.
- **Flaw: Compulsive (Gambling)** & **Debt** — The very trait that makes Wynn valuable also keeps Wynn leveraged; the desk considers "significant personal debt" not a disqualifier but a *management tool*.

## Description & Demeanor
Charming, quick, immaculately dressed, and always three sentences ahead in a negotiation — the kind of person who buys the round and somehow leaves richer. Wynn treats the whole apparatus of Consensus Holdings as the greatest game ever devised and hasn't yet let themselves ask who's actually winning it.

## What They Want
To land the assignment nobody else will touch, clear the debt on a single perfect trade, and be *promoted upward into the part of the company that clearly exists but nobody will name.*

## Secret
Wynn has noticed that the win rate is not luck — that on the bets Wynn "intends to win," the outcome bends. Wynn has told no one, is terrified it will be either recruited or corrected the moment it's confirmed, and has started deliberately losing small wagers to hide the size of the anomaly.
